Overview of Vedanta

Vedanta is one of the six orthodox schools of Hindu philosophy, based on the teachings found in the Upanishads, the Brahma Sutras, and the Bhagavad Gita. It focuses on the nature of reality, the self, and the relationship between the individual and the ultimate reality, Brahman.

Core Teachings

  • Brahman is the ultimate reality.
  • Atman (soul) is identical to Brahman.
  • The world is an illusion (Maya).

Practice and Realization

Vedanta encourages the study of scriptures, meditation, and the pursuit of knowledge to attain Moksha, or liberation.
